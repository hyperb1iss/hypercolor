Hypercolor is an open-source RGB lighting orchestration engine for Linux, written in Rust. It renders HTML/Canvas effects at 60fps, samples pixels at LED positions using spatial mapping, and pushes color data to every RGB device in your setup: Razer peripherals, WLED strips, ASUS motherboards, PrismRGB controllers, Corsair gear, and more.

Whether you're a PC builder who wants audio-reactive case lighting, a developer who wants to author custom effects in TypeScript, or someone who just wants their RGB to stop looking like a unicorn vomited on their desk, this guide will get you running.

In this section you'll learn how to:

- **Install** Hypercolor and its system dependencies
- **Start the daemon** and connect your first device
- **Apply effects** through the web UI, CLI, or REST API
- **Configure** profiles, audio input, and device mappings
