# Commercial Posture and Open Source Promise

This document describes Hypercolor's licensing, contribution terms, and commercial posture.

---

## License

Hypercolor is licensed under the **Apache License, Version 2.0**. See [LICENSE](LICENSE).

Apache-2.0 is a permissive open-source license. You can use, copy, modify, merge, publish, distribute, sublicense, and sell the software and derivative works, subject to attribution and the license notice requirements in the Apache-2.0 terms. The full text is in the LICENSE file.

Hypercolor will remain open source. There is no plan to relicense, add a proprietary tier to the core engine, or move existing functionality behind a paywall.

---

## Contribution Terms

**Inbound = outbound.** When you submit a contribution (code, documentation, effect, driver, or anything else), you license it to the project under the same Apache-2.0 terms. This is the standard open-source contribution model.

There is **no Contributor License Agreement (CLA)**. You keep your copyright. You grant the project a license to use your contribution under Apache-2.0, the same license the project uses.

By submitting a pull request you represent that you have the right to submit the contribution under these terms, consistent with the [Developer Certificate of Origin](https://developercertificate.org/).

## What Is Settled

To be clear about what is not a pending decision:

- The core engine is open source under Apache-2.0 and will stay that way.
- Contributions are inbound = outbound, no CLA.
- The project is maintained by Stefanie Jane (stef@hyperbliss.tech).
- Community funding goes toward hardware for driver development and development time. It does not confer special rights or access.
